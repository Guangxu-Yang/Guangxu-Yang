Simulation methods in communication complexity, revisited

(with Jiapeng Zhang)

The notion of lifting theorems is a generic method to lift the hardness of one-party functions to two-party lower bounds in the communication model. It has many applications in different areas such as proof complexity, game theory, and combinatorial optimization. Among many lifting results, a central idea is called the Raz-McKenize simulation (FOCS 1997). This simulation provides a systematic way to convert a communication protocol into a corresponding decision tree. Though it is very convenient in many applications, there are still some challenges in this framework. A major problem is that the Raz-McKenize simulation requires a very large gadget. In this paper, we revise the Raz-McKenzie simulation and introduce a density increment argument to prove communication lower bounds. Our contribution is three-fold:

  • We give a simple alternative proof of \(\Omega(n)\) corruption lower bound of set disjointness.

  • We prove a block sensitivity to corruption bound lifting theorem with constant-size gadgets.

  • We introduce a quantum version of corruption bound and prove an average sensitivity to quantum corruption bound lifting theorem with constant-size gadgets.

Besides the result itself, we believe our density increment arguments and the definition of quantum corruption bound may have more applications in diverse areas. We also discuss why our method has the potential to avoid the large-size gadget bottleneck in the Raz-McKenzie simulation.
