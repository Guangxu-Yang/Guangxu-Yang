Lifting theorems meet information complexity: set disjointness

(with Jiapeng Zhang)

Information complexity and lifting theorems are two most powerful frameworks with many applications in different areas. However, the connections between information complexity and lifting theorems are less clear. We adopt the idea of Raz-McKenzieâ€™s simulation (FOCS 1997) to obtain new proofs for randomized \(\Omega(n)\) communication lower boound of set disjointness and optimal \(\Omega(n/k)\) lower bound on the deterministic(and randomized) communication complexity of the k-party set disjointness function with the unique intersection promise in the blackboard model which give a unified, less ad-hoc way of proving communication lower bounds for \(k\text{-}\mathrm{UDISJ}\). Our proofs may firstly build the connections between corruption bound, information complexity and lifting theorems.

Simulation methods in communication complexity, revisited

(with Jiapeng Zhang)

We revise Raz-McKenzie simulation. We introduce a white-box simulation, proving lifting theorems for block sensitivity with constant-size gadgets. Concretely, we show there is a constant-size gadget \(g\) such that for any Boolean function \(f\), the corruption bound of \(f\circ g^n\) is lower bounded by \(\Omega(\mathrm{bs}(f))\). Combined with a result of Beame et al. (CCC 2005), this implies the randomized communication complexity of \(f\circ g^n\) is lower bounded by \(\Omega(\mathrm{bs}(f))\). Besides the result itself, we believe our simulation technique may have more applications in diverse areas. We also discuss why our simulation method has a potential to avoid the large-size gadget bottleneck in Raz-McKenzie simulation.

Quantum lifting theorem via a simulation method

(with Xudong Wu)

We propose a new simulation method, proving quantum lifting theorems for total influence. Concretely, we show there is a constant \(C\) such that for any Boolean function \(f:\{0,1\}^n \rightarrow \{0,1\}\)and low discrepancy gadget \(g:[q]^n\times [q]^n \rightarrow \{0,1\}\) with \(q\geq C\), the bound-error quantum communication complexity of \(f\circ g^n\) is lower bounded by \(\Omega(\log q\cdot \bar{\mathrm{Inf}}(f))\). Best of our knowledge, it's the first quantum lifting theorem proved via a simulation method, we believe our simulation technique may have more applications in proving quantum lifting theorems.
