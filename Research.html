Block-sensitivity to Communication Lifting Theorem

(with Jiapeng Zhang)

Lifting theorems are a generic way to lift hardness of boolean functions to lower bounds in communication complexity, with many applications in different areas, such as proof complexity, game theory, combinatorial optimization.The lifting theorem for the block-sensitivity has been well-studied in previous papers via reduction [Zhang09][GP14] or information complexity approach [HN12]. In this paper, we prove a lifting theorem for the block-sensitivity by white box simulation technique. We show that \(\mathbf{P}^{\mathrm{cc}}(f\circ g^n) = \Omega(q\cdot \mathrm{bs}(f))\) and \(\mathbf{BPP}^{\mathrm{cc}}(f\circ g^n) = \Omega(\log q\cdot \mathrm{bs}(f))\) for any for all gadgets \(g:[q]\times [q] \rightarrow \{0,1\}\) with \(q > 2\) that have exponentially-small discrepancy. We also provide an alternate proof of \(\Omega(n/k)\) lower bound on the derministic(and randomized) communication complexity of the k-party disjointness function with the unique intersection promise.

A Disperser Property of Lifting for Equality Gadget.

Disperser property play an important role in deterministic lfting theorem [LMMPZ20]. As lifting theorem for equality gadget [LM18][dRMN+20] received more attention in recent years, whether there is a disperser property of equality gadget is still open. In this paper, we prove a disperser property of equality gadget. As a result, we present an decision tree interpretation for the proof of FORK game [GS91] by using path simulation technique and the disperser property of equality gadget. The disperser property of equality gadget gives a new perspective on previous proof and lifting theorem for equality gadget.
