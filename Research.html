Lifting Theorems Meet Information Complexity: Set Disjointness

(with Jiapeng Zhang and Jack DePascale)

Information complexity and lifting theorems are two most powerful frameworks with many applications in different areas. However, the connections between information complexity and lifting theorems are less clear. In this paper, we adopt the idea of Raz-McKenzie’s simulation [RM99,GPW15] to obtain new proofs for randomized \(\Omega(n)\) communication lower boound of set disjointness and optimal \(\Omega(n/k)\) lower bound on the deterministic(and randomized) communication complexity of the k-party set disjointness function with the unique intersection promise in the blackboard model which give a unified, less ad-hoc way of proving communication lower bounds for \(k\text{-}\mathrm{UDISJ}\). Our proofs may firstly build the connections between corruption bound, information complexity and lifting theorems.

Block Sensitivity to Information Complexity Lifting Theorems

(with Jiapeng Zhang and Jack DePascale)

Lifting theorems are a generic way to lift hardness of boolean functions to lower bounds in communication complexity, with many applications in different areas, such as proof complexity, game theory, combinatorial optimization. The lifting theorem for the block-sensitivity has been well-studied in previous papers via reduction [Zhang09,GP14] or information complexity approach [HN12]. In this paper, based on Raz-McKenzie’s simulation [RM99,GPW15], we propose a white box simulation technique. We prove a lifting theorem for block sensitivity via the white box simulation technique. We show that \(\mathbf{P}^{\mathrm{cc}}(f\circ g^n) = \Omega(\log q\cdot \mathrm{bs}(f))\) and \(\mathbf{IC}^{\mathrm{ext}}(f\circ g^n) = \Omega(\log q\cdot \mathrm{bs}(f))\) for any for all gadgets \(g:[q]\times [q] \rightarrow \{0,1\}\) with \(q > 2\) that have exponentially-small discrepancy. Our white box simulation technique may be independent of interest.
