Communication Lower Bounds in Cryptography via Density Incremental Arguments

(with Mi-Ying (Miryam) Huang,Xinyu Mao and Jiapeng Zhang)

Communication is an important resource in many cryptographic protocols. In this work, we study the communication complexity of key-agreement protocols. We focus on key-agreement protocols in the random oracle model (ROM), which is an alternative to protocols based on public-key cryptography. In ROM, the parties and the eavesdropper have access to a random function (an “oracle”), but the parties are limited in the number of queries they can make to the oracle. It is conjectured that (e.g., Haitner et al. (ITCS 2019)), in order to insure security against an eavesdropper that makes roughly \(c\ell\) queries, the honest parties need to exchange \(\Omega(c)\) bits, where \(\ell\) is the number of queries made by honest parties. This conjectured bound matches the constructions by Merkle (Communications of the ACM 1978). We prove the conjecture in the case where protocols are non-adaptive with perfect completeness. On the technical side, we adopt the density increment arguments into the random oracle model setting.
