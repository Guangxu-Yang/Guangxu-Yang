Tight Communication Lower Bounds for Collision Problems via Density Increment Arguments

with Tal Roth and Jiapeng Zhang

The collision problems are important problems in complexity theory and cryptography with wide applications. In this paper, we show the optimal $\Omega(N^{1/4})$ randomized communication lower bound for the natural and well-defined collision problems in the two-party communication setting. For applications, we leverage these lower bounds to achieve several new results:

  • We improve the lower bound for a weak bit-pigeonhole search problem in Goos and Jain (RANDOM 2022) which also answers a question of Itsykson and Riazanov (CCC 2021).

  • We prove the conjecture in Bauer and Farshim et al (CRYPTO 2018) which implies the collision resistance for the combiner of backdoored random oracles.

Our proof uses a novel idea called the density increment argument which can be viewed as a new technique for proving communication lower bounds under product distributions and bypassing some barriers in information complexity and lifting theorems.
